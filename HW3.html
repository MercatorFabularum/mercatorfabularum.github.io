"How Can England Reclaim Their Moors?"

Written by: Richard Collett


Most recently from the BBC, the article “How can England reclaim its moors?” is an article that shows the cultural difference between the United Kingdom and the United States. Written by Richard Collett, the article tells of the battle that the people had to fight to maintain the right to camp and hike and free roam over Dartmoor National Park.




For decades, Dartmoor National Park has been one of the few places in England that protects the rights of people to freely roam the park. Similarly in the U.S., people must get permission otherwise to be on another person's property. In our country, this law would not have any conflict regarding our national parks. However, unlike in the United States, people can buy land in the national park and therefore making it their private property and all the rights to it.



These conflicting laws have never been an issue until Alexander Darwall stopped allowing backpackers on his property. This led to a ruling by the High Court of England last January that gave Dartmoor landowners the right to stop public access to their lands. Citizens and visitors alike were furious about the ruling. National associations like the Open Spaces Society, Dartmoor Preservation Association, and Dartmoor National Park Authority worked hard to change the ruling. A protest on Darwall's property also occurred and was one of the largest protests in England. The people of England came together to protect their right to Dartmoor Park, a right that has gained importance after the COVID pandemic. In the end, the people of England were able to successfully get an appeal to the original ruling leading to free roaming on Dartmoor once again.
